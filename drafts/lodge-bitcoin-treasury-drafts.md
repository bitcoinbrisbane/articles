## Option A: The Poker Player's Take (Reddit tone — punchy, personal, conversational)

**Title:** I played poker at The Lodge weeks before the raid. Here's why every business needs a Bitcoin treasury.

I visited The Lodge Card Club in Round Rock before Consensus. If you don't know it — it's the largest poker room in Texas, nearly 70 tables, co-owned by Doug Polk, Brad Owen, and Andrew Neeme. The kind of place where the crypto and poker worlds overlap in the best way.

On March 10, [TABC's Financial Crimes Unit raided The Lodge](https://www.pokernews.com/news/2026/03/lodge-card-club-texas-poker-room-raided-50786.htm). Approximately 20 agents, plus the Williamson County Sheriff's Office. Players were told to take their chips home without cashing out.

No arrests. [No charges filed](https://www.casino.org/news/money-laundering-illegal-gambling-probe-behind-raid-on-austins-the-lodge-card-club/). The Lodge's own attorneys said the ["situation has not been explained to us."](https://www.pokernews.com/news/2026/03/lodge-card-club-poker-room-raid-statement-50794.htm)

Let that sink in. A legal business, operating in the open, gets its assets frozen and its doors shut — without anyone being charged with anything.

Doug Polk called it a ["witch hunt"](https://www.pokernews.com/news/2026/03/doug-polk-address-lodge-poker-raid-50793.htm) and personally guaranteed player funds. But here's the thing — he shouldn't have to. Your assets shouldn't be seized before you've been convicted of a crime. That's not how due process works.

**This is exactly why businesses should hold Bitcoin.**

When your treasury sits in a bank account, it can be frozen with a single warrant. No conviction. No trial. No explanation — as The Lodge just learned. Civil asset forfeiture in the US allows the government to [seize property without ever charging the owner with a crime](https://harvardlawreview.org/print/vol-131/how-crime-pays-the-unconstitutionality-of-modern-civil-asset-forfeiture-as-a-tool-of-criminal-law-enforcement/). The burden of proof flips — *you* have to prove your assets are "innocent."

Bitcoin in a properly secured wallet can't be frozen by a state agency executing a warrant on your bank. It can't be held indefinitely while bureaucrats decide whether to file charges. Self-custody means self-sovereignty.

This isn't theoretical anymore. [Over 170 publicly traded companies now hold Bitcoin on their balance sheets](https://coinmarketcap.com/charts/bitcoin-treasuries/), led by Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) with [over 717,000 BTC](https://www.demandsage.com/public-companies-holding-btc/). They're not doing it because they're crypto bros. They're doing it because they understand counterparty risk — and a government that can freeze your accounts without charges is the ultimate counterparty.

The Lodge hasn't been convicted of anything. They may have done nothing wrong. But their business is shut and their assets are frozen regardless. If that doesn't make the case for a Bitcoin treasury, I don't know what does.

## Option B: The Constitutional Argument (LinkedIn tone — professional, measured, policy-focused)

**Title:** The Lodge Card Club Raid and the Case for Corporate Bitcoin Treasuries

On March 10, the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission's Financial Crimes Unit [raided The Lodge Card Club](https://www.pokernews.com/news/2026/03/lodge-card-club-texas-poker-room-raided-50786.htm) in Round Rock, Texas — the state's largest poker room, with nearly 70 tables and co-owned by poker professionals Doug Polk, Brad Owen, and Andrew Neeme. Approximately 20 agents, along with officers from the Williamson County Sheriff's Office, executed a search and seizure warrant.

I'd visited The Lodge shortly before Consensus. It's a professionally run operation in a state where poker rooms operate legally. What happened next should concern every business owner.

[No arrests were made. No charges have been filed.](https://www.casino.org/news/money-laundering-illegal-gambling-probe-behind-raid-on-austins-the-lodge-card-club/) The Lodge's legal counsel stated the ["situation has not been explained to us."](https://www.pokernews.com/news/2026/03/lodge-card-club-poker-room-raid-statement-50794.htm) Yet the business was effectively shut down, the [WPT postponed its scheduled event](https://www.gamblinginsider.com/news/117311/wpt-postpones-lodge-event-texas-raid), and player funds were frozen.

This is civil asset forfeiture in practice. As the [Harvard Law Review has documented](https://harvardlawreview.org/print/vol-131/how-crime-pays-the-unconstitutionality-of-modern-civil-asset-forfeiture-as-a-tool-of-criminal-law-enforcement/), modern civil forfeiture allows the government to seize property without criminal charges, effectively reversing the burden of proof. The Fourth Amendment guarantees the right to be secure against unreasonable seizures. The Fifth Amendment guarantees due process. Civil asset forfeiture, as [critics across the political spectrum argue](https://www.ipi.org/ipi_issues/detail/civil-asset-forfeiture-and-the-constitution), circumvents both.

**The business implication is straightforward: if your operating capital sits in accounts that can be frozen without due process, your business is one warrant away from shutdown.**

This is the strongest case yet for corporate Bitcoin treasuries. Not as speculation — as operational resilience. Bitcoin held in self-custody cannot be frozen by a state agency. It cannot be seized without physical access to private keys. It operates outside the traditional banking chokepoints that make civil forfeiture so efficient.

The trend is already underway. [Over 170 publicly traded companies](https://coinmarketcap.com/charts/bitcoin-treasuries/) now hold Bitcoin, with Strategy holding [over 717,000 BTC](https://www.demandsage.com/public-companies-holding-btc/) and firms like Marathon, Twenty One Capital, and Japan's Metaplanet building significant positions. These companies cite inflation hedging and balance sheet diversification. But the under-discussed reason may be the most compelling: **reducing exposure to arbitrary asset seizure.**

The Lodge may or may not face charges in the future. That's for the legal process to determine. But the fact that a legal business can be shut down and its assets frozen *before* that process even begins should give every business operator pause — and a reason to reconsider where they hold their reserves.

Not your keys, not your coins. Not your keys, not your business.
